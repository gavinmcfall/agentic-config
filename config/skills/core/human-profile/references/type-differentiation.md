# Type Differentiation Guide

This reference helps the interviewer distinguish between all 16 MBTI types diagnostically. The goal is not to confirm a suspected type but to narrow down which type best fits the person.

## Cognitive Function Stacks

Every type uses four cognitive functions in a specific order. Understanding this stack is essential for accurate typing — two types can share all four letters except one and have completely different function stacks.

### The Eight Functions

| Function | Code | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Extraverted Intuition | Ne | Sees possibilities, patterns, connections between ideas. Divergent — explores many options. |
| Introverted Intuition | Ni | Converges on insight, sees where things are heading. "Just knows" things without clear reasoning path. |
| Extraverted Sensing | Se | Fully present in the physical moment. Notices sensory details, acts on what's immediately real. |
| Introverted Sensing | Si | Compares present to past experience. Stores detailed memories, values tradition and reliability. |
| Extraverted Thinking | Te | Organizes the external world. Efficiency, systems, measurable results, objective standards. |
| Introverted Thinking | Ti | Builds internal logical frameworks. Precision, consistency, understanding how things work. |
| Extraverted Feeling | Fe | Reads and responds to group emotional climate. Harmony, social awareness, others' needs. |
| Introverted Feeling | Fi | Deep personal values and authenticity. Knows what feels right/wrong based on internal moral compass. |

## Key Differentiator Questions

### The Four Dichotomies

Each dichotomy has a core question that's more diagnostic than the typical surface-level check.

#### Introversion vs Extraversion (Energy Direction)

**Surface question** (weak): "Do you like parties?"
**Diagnostic question** (strong): "After spending 3 hours helping a friend through a crisis — something you genuinely wanted to do — do you feel energized or depleted? What do you need next?"

Why this works: Both introverts and extraverts can enjoy parties or dislike them. The diagnostic difference is about energy — where it comes FROM, not what you do with it. Introverts are depleted by social engagement even when it's meaningful and wanted. Extraverts are energized.

**Additional differentiators:**
- "When you have a complex problem to solve, do you prefer to think it through alone first, or talk it through with someone?" (Processing mode)
- "In a group discussion, do you naturally speak up as ideas form, or do you prefer to have your thoughts fully formed before sharing?" (Idea development — internal vs external)
- "Do you ever feel lonely when alone, or do you more often feel overstimulated when with people?" (Default discomfort direction)

#### Sensing vs Intuition (Information Processing)

**Surface question** (weak): "Are you detail-oriented or big-picture?"
**Diagnostic question** (strong): "When you walk into an unfamiliar room, what do you notice first — the specific physical details (color, layout, temperature), or the overall vibe and what it reminds you of?"

Why this works: Sensors lead with concrete physical data. Intuitives lead with patterns, associations, and abstract impressions. Both can eventually access the other mode, but the FIRST instinct reveals the preference.

**Additional differentiators:**
- "When someone gives you instructions, do you prefer step-by-step specifics, or do you prefer understanding the overall goal so you can figure out the steps yourself?" (Information trust)
- "Do you tend to think about what IS (concrete present reality) or what COULD BE (future possibilities)?" (Time orientation)
- "When learning something new, do you trust your direct experience more, or do you trust patterns and theories more?" (Epistemology)
- "Do metaphors and analogies help you understand things, or do you prefer literal, concrete explanations?" (Language processing)

#### Thinking vs Feeling (Decision Making)

**Surface question** (weak): "Are you logical or emotional?"
**Diagnostic question** (strong): "A close friend asks for honest feedback on a project they've poured their heart into. It has a serious flaw. Walk me through how you'd handle that conversation."

Why this works: Both thinkers and feelers can be emotional or logical. The difference is in how they make decisions and prioritize. Thinkers prioritize the truth/principle even when it's uncomfortable. Feelers prioritize the impact on the person and the relationship, even when the truth is clear.

**Additional differentiators:**
- "When making a tough decision, do you weigh what's objectively fair/correct, or what's best for the people involved?" (Decision basis)
- "Do you value being respected for your competence, or being valued for who you are as a person?" (Validation preference)
- "When you disagree with someone, is your first instinct to prove your point or to understand theirs?" (Conflict approach)
- "Is it more important to be truthful or to be kind? Or is that a false dichotomy for you?" (Values hierarchy)

#### Judging vs Perceiving (Lifestyle Orientation)

**Surface question** (weak): "Are you organized?"
**Diagnostic question** (strong): "You're planning a two-week vacation. Do you research and book everything in advance, or do you prefer to arrive with minimal plans and figure it out as you go? How do you FEEL about each approach?"

Why this works: Many perceivers are organized (especially IxTPs with strong Ti systems). Many judgers can be spontaneous. The diagnostic difference is about closure and openness — do they feel better when things are decided and planned, or when options remain open?

**Additional differentiators:**
- "When a project deadline approaches, do you feel energized by the time pressure, or stressed?" (Deadline relationship)
- "Do you prefer to finish one thing before starting another, or do you naturally juggle multiple things?" (Closure need)
- "If plans change at the last minute, is your first feeling relief (new possibilities) or frustration (disruption)?" (Change response)
- "Do you work steadily toward deadlines or tend to do your best work in bursts near the end?" (Work rhythm)

### Assertive vs Turbulent (Identity — 16Personalities specific)

This isn't a classical MBTI dichotomy but 16Personalities adds it as a 5th dimension.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- "When you make a mistake, how long does it stay with you? Do you move on quickly or replay it?" (Self-criticism duration)
- "Do you tend to worry about things that haven't happened yet?" (Anticipatory anxiety)
- "Are you your own harshest critic, or do you give yourself the same grace you'd give a friend?" (Self-compassion)
- "Does your sense of self-worth fluctuate based on external feedback, or is it relatively stable?" (Identity stability)

**Assertive**: Stable self-image, handles stress calmly, doesn't dwell on mistakes, resistant to pressure.
**Turbulent**: Self-conscious, driven by perfectionism, uses stress as fuel, sensitive to feedback, eager to improve.

## Commonly Confused Type Pairs

These pairs are most frequently mistyped as each other. For each, key differentiating questions are provided.

### INFJ vs INFP

This is the most commonly confused pair. Both are idealistic, introspective, value-driven introverts. The difference is fundamental:

- **INFJ** (Ni-Fe): Focuses on others' emotions and societal patterns. Their values come from understanding what others need. They have a vision of how things SHOULD be and work toward it. Organized about external commitments.
- **INFP** (Fi-Ne): Focuses on internal authenticity and personal values. Their values come from deep within. They explore many possibilities for self-expression. May appear disorganized externally.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When you're in a group and the mood shifts, do you naturally adjust your behavior to manage the group's emotional state, or do you stay true to your own emotional state regardless?" (Fe vs Fi)
2. "Do you have a strong sense of where things are heading — an almost prophetic certainty — or do you prefer to keep exploring possibilities without converging?" (Ni convergence vs Ne divergence)
3. "Is your sense of right and wrong based more on how actions affect others (Fe) or on an internal moral code that exists regardless of others' opinions (Fi)?"
4. "Do you find it easier to articulate what others are feeling (Fe) or what you are feeling (Fi)?"
5. "When you disagree with someone in an argument, do you tend to back down to keep peace (Fe — INFJ), or hold your position because it's authentically what you believe (Fi — INFP)?"
6. "When explaining something important to you, do you use minimal context and expect others to follow your leaps (INFJ — Ni shorthand), or do you over-explain to make sure your inner world is fully understood (INFP — Fi needs to be seen accurately)?"

**Behavioral markers:**
- INFJs often appear more "put together" externally — Fe creates social smoothness even when inner life is turbulent
- INFPs may struggle more to articulate feelings in real-time because Fi processes internally first
- INFJs write/speak to connect with the audience; INFPs write/speak to be authentic to themselves
- ~80% of people who self-type as INFJ are actually INFP, ISFJ, or ISFP — this is the most over-claimed type

### INTJ vs INFJ

Both lead with Ni — they share the "just knowing" intuition, convergent thinking, and long-range vision. The difference is in their second function.

- **INTJ** (Ni-Te): Combines vision with systematic execution. Values efficiency and competence. Wants to build effective systems.
- **INFJ** (Ni-Fe): Combines vision with emotional intelligence. Values harmony and understanding. Wants to help people fulfill their potential.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When managing a team, is your primary concern that the work is excellent and efficient, or that the people feel valued and understood?"
2. "Do you make decisions primarily based on logical analysis, or do you weigh how the decision will affect the people involved?"
3. "When someone at work is underperforming, is your first instinct to analyze why the system is failing, or to understand what the person is going through?"
4. "When you receive criticism, do you evaluate it against objective standards (Te — INTJ), or does it sting because of how it makes you feel about the relationship (Fe — INFJ)?"

**Behavioral markers:**
- INTJs can appear cold but have deep Fi feelings they rarely share — look for private emotional intensity
- INFJs can appear analytical but their reasoning serves people — look for whether conclusions prioritize systems or humans

### INFJ vs ISFJ

Both use Fe and are caring, attentive to others. The difference is Si (past-oriented, detail-storing) vs Ni (pattern-seeking, future-oriented).

- **ISFJ** (Si-Fe): Loyal, detail-oriented, preserves what works. Remembers specific past experiences vividly. Practical caring — shows love through concrete actions.
- **INFJ** (Ni-Fe): Visionary, pattern-oriented, seeks meaning. Has sudden insights about the future. Abstract caring — shows love through understanding and guidance.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "Do you tend to remember the specific details of past events (what was said, what people wore) or the overall impression and meaning of what happened?"
2. "When helping someone, do you focus on practical concrete support (food, logistics, being present) or on helping them see the deeper meaning or path forward?"
3. "Do you trust proven methods and established procedures, or do you prefer to find new approaches even when old ones work?"
4. "When you think about the future, do you see specific concrete plans (Si — ISFJ) or abstract patterns of where things are heading (Ni — INFJ)?"

**Critical note:** ISFJs are the most common mistype for INFJ. ISFJs often test as INFJ because they are caring, insightful about people, and value depth — but their insight comes from accumulated experience (Si), not pattern recognition (Ni). Ask for the SOURCE of their "knowing" — is it "I've seen this before" or "I just know"?

### INTP vs INTJ

Both are analytical introverts. They look similar from the outside but think very differently.

- **INTP** (Ti-Ne): Explores ideas for the sake of understanding. Values internal logical consistency. Keeps options open, hates premature closure.
- **INTJ** (Ni-Te): Converges on a vision and executes. Values external effectiveness. Decisive, seeks closure, builds toward goals.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When you encounter an interesting idea, do you want to explore it from every angle (even impractical ones) or do you immediately assess its practical application?"
2. "Is it more frustrating to be forced into a premature decision, or to have an open-ended situation with no clear path forward?"
3. "Are you more interested in understanding HOW something works (mechanism) or THAT it works effectively (result)?"
4. "How do you feel about change? Do you see it as an opportunity for improvement (Ni-Te — INTJ) or as something that disrupts understanding (Ti-Ne — INTP)?"
5. "When you imagine the future, is it concrete and specific — a clear destination (INTJ) — or more abstract and theoretical (INTP)?"

### INTP vs ISTP

Both lead with Ti — internal logical analysis. The difference is Ne (abstract possibilities) vs Se (concrete physical reality).

- **INTP** (Ti-Ne): Theoretical. Lives in the world of ideas and possibilities. May struggle with physical/practical tasks.
- **ISTP** (Ti-Se): Practical. Applies logical analysis to the physical world. Hands-on problem solver, good in crises.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When you analyze a problem, do you tend to think in abstractions and hypotheticals, or do you focus on what you can see, touch, and test in the real world?"
2. "Are you more drawn to theoretical physics or mechanical engineering? (Or equivalent: understanding why vs making it work)"
3. "In a crisis, do you need a moment to think through all scenarios, or do you act instinctively based on what's in front of you?"
4. "When something is broken, do you want to take it apart and fix it with your hands (ISTP), or do you want to read about how it works first (INTP)?"
5. "Do you learn best from hands-on experimentation (Se — ISTP) or from reading and theoretical exploration (Ne — INTP)?"

### ENFP vs ENTP

Both lead with Ne — they explore possibilities, brainstorm freely, and generate ideas rapidly. The difference is values-driven (Fi) vs logic-driven (Ti).

- **ENFP** (Ne-Fi): Explores possibilities through a values lens. Cares deeply about authenticity and personal meaning. Motivated by what feels right.
- **ENTP** (Ne-Ti): Explores possibilities through a logic lens. Cares about intellectual truth and elegant systems. Motivated by what makes sense.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When debating, do you argue for what you believe is RIGHT (values), or do you enjoy arguing any side just to explore the logic?"
2. "Is it more important that your work aligns with your personal values, or that it's intellectually stimulating?"
3. "When someone shares a new idea, do you first evaluate how it sits with your values, or do you first evaluate its logical consistency?"

### ENFJ vs ESFJ

Both lead with Fe — harmony-seeking, people-oriented, attentive to group needs. The difference is Ni (future patterns) vs Si (past experience).

- **ENFJ** (Fe-Ni): Reads the room AND sees where people/situations are heading. Visionary leader who inspires toward a future state. May neglect present details for future vision.
- **ESFJ** (Fe-Si): Reads the room AND remembers what's worked before. Practical caretaker who maintains traditions and stability. May resist change in favor of proven methods.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When organizing an event, do you focus more on creating a new, inspiring experience, or on making sure everything runs smoothly based on what's worked before?"
2. "Do you motivate people by painting a picture of the future, or by reminding them of shared values and traditions?"
3. "Are you more interested in where people COULD be, or in making sure they're okay right NOW?"
4. "When you talk about an experience, do you tend toward metaphorical language — 'it felt like a wave' (ENFJ — Ni) — or literal, concrete language — 'we sat in the garden and she told me about her week' (ESFJ — Si)?"
5. "How do you relax? ENFJs tend toward cognitive rest (daydreaming, exploring ideas); ESFJs tend toward physical comfort (cooking, gardening, familiar routines)."
6. "How do you feel about change? ENFJs welcome it as growth; ESFJs accept it when it serves people but prefer stability."

### ISTJ vs ISFJ

Both lead with Si — detail-oriented, reliable, tradition-respecting introverts. The difference is Te (impersonal logic) vs Fe (interpersonal harmony).

- **ISTJ** (Si-Te): Follows rules because they're logical and efficient. Values competence and accuracy. May seem cold when enforcing standards.
- **ISFJ** (Si-Fe): Follows rules because they maintain harmony and care for others. Values relationships and reliability. May sacrifice efficiency for people's feelings.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When someone breaks a rule, is your first reaction 'that's wrong and inefficient' or 'that could hurt someone or damage trust'?"
2. "At work, is it more important that processes are optimized, or that people feel supported?"
3. "When you remember past events, do you focus on the facts and sequences, or on how people felt?"

### ESTJ vs ENTJ

Both use Te — they organize, direct, and demand efficiency. The difference is Si (experience-based) vs Ni (vision-based).

- **ESTJ** (Te-Si): Manages based on proven methods. "This is how it's done." Values consistency and reliability.
- **ENTJ** (Te-Ni): Manages based on strategic vision. "This is where we're going." Values innovation and long-term results.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When leading a team, do you rely more on established procedures that have proven results, or do you push for new strategies based on your vision of what could work?"
2. "Do you trust experience and track record, or insight and strategic thinking?"
3. "Is it more frustrating when people ignore proven methods, or when they resist a clearly better new approach?"

### ESTP vs ESFP

Both lead with Se — action-oriented, present-focused, physical-world engaged. The difference is Ti (impersonal analysis) vs Fi (personal values).

- **ESTP** (Se-Ti): Applies logical analysis to the immediate environment. Strategic, competitive, seeks to understand systems to gain advantage.
- **ESFP** (Se-Fi): Applies personal values to the immediate environment. Warm, expressive, seeks authentic experiences that align with who they are.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When making a quick decision, do you go with what makes logical sense, or what feels right in your gut?"
2. "In a conflict, do you focus on who's objectively correct, or on how it's affecting the people involved?"
3. "Are you more motivated by winning/mastering a challenge, or by having meaningful, authentic experiences?"
4. "When you describe something exciting, do you use analytical language — 'it was a well-designed system' (ESTP — Ti) — or expressive, values-laden language — 'it was beautiful and real' (ESFP — Fi)?"

**Decisive differentiator:** Language style. ESFPs tend toward literal but emotionally rich descriptions. ESTPs tend toward strategic, analytical descriptions of the same experiences.

### ISFP vs INFP

Both lead with Fi — deep internal values, authenticity-seeking, sensitive to personal meaning. The difference is Se (present physical) vs Ne (future possibilities).

- **ISFP** (Fi-Se): Values expressed through physical action and art. Lives in the present moment. Creates tangible beauty and meaning.
- **INFP** (Fi-Ne): Values expressed through ideas and possibilities. Lives in the world of potential. Creates abstract meaning and explores what could be.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "Do you express yourself better through physical creation (art, cooking, fashion, physical action) or through writing, ideas, and imagination?"
2. "Are you more grounded in the present moment, or do you spend more time in your imagination?"
3. "When something matters to you, do you prefer to act on it concretely now, or think through all the possible ways it could unfold?"

## Stress/Grip Patterns by Type

Under extreme stress, each type falls into the "grip" of their inferior (4th) function. This produces uncharacteristic behavior that can help with typing — if someone describes acting very unlike themselves under stress, the stressed behavior often points to their inferior function.

| Type | Inferior | Grip Behavior |
|------|----------|--------------|
| INTJ | Se | Overindulges in sensory pleasures, becomes reckless with physical world |
| INTP | Fe | Becomes oversensitive to others' opinions, seeks validation inappropriately |
| ENTJ | Fi | Becomes hypersensitive, self-pitying, feels misunderstood, emotional outbursts |
| ENTP | Si | Becomes fixated on physical symptoms, nostalgic, rigid about routines |
| INFJ | Se | Overindulges in sensory pleasures, becomes impulsive, physical excess |
| INFP | Te | Becomes harshly critical, judgmental, obsessed with external metrics |
| ENFJ | Ti | Becomes coldly analytical, overly critical, loses warmth |
| ENFP | Si | Becomes rigid, detail-obsessed, catastrophizes from past failures |
| ISTJ | Ne | Catastrophizes about possibilities, sees danger everywhere, paranoid thinking |
| ISFJ | Ne | Imagines worst-case scenarios, anxious about change, sees threats in possibilities |
| ESTJ | Fi | Becomes emotionally overwhelmed, self-pitying, feels attacked, manipulative |
| ESFJ | Ti | Becomes coldly analytical, critical, cynical, loses empathy |
| ISTP | Fe | Becomes emotional, needy, oversensitive to relationship dynamics |
| ISFP | Te | Becomes controlling, rigid about rules, harshly critical |
| ESTP | Ni | Becomes paranoid about future, sees hidden meanings everywhere, conspiracy thinking |
| ESFP | Ni | Becomes anxious about the future, sees dark symbolism, feels trapped by fate |

## Typing Methodology

### Step 1: Establish the Big Dichotomies
Phases 1-3 of the interview should clearly establish I/E, S/N, and J/P. These are usually the most observable.

### Step 2: Narrow T/F
Phase 4 and 6 reveal the decision-making preference. This is often the hardest to pin down because cultural expectations (men "should" be T, women "should" be F) create masking.

### Step 3: Verify with Function Stack
Once you have a 4-letter hypothesis, verify against the cognitive function stack. Does their dominant function match the one predicted by the type code? If not, reconsider.

**Quick verification**: Ask about their "default mode of thinking" — what their mind does when idle:
- **Ni-doms** (INTJ, INFJ): Mind converges on insights, sees patterns, "just knows"
- **Ne-doms** (ENTP, ENFP): Mind generates possibilities, makes connections, jumps between ideas
- **Si-doms** (ISTJ, ISFJ): Mind recalls past experiences in vivid detail, compares present to past
- **Se-doms** (ESTP, ESFP): Mind is fully present, notices physical environment, ready to act
- **Ti-doms** (INTP, ISTP): Mind analyzes logical frameworks, finds inconsistencies, builds models
- **Te-doms** (ENTJ, ESTJ): Mind organizes and optimizes, plans execution, measures efficiency
- **Fi-doms** (INFP, ISFP): Mind evaluates against personal values, feels deeply about authenticity
- **Fe-doms** (ENFJ, ESFJ): Mind reads social dynamics, considers others' needs, seeks harmony

### Step 4: Check Stress Patterns
Ask about uncharacteristic behavior under extreme stress. The grip pattern should match the inferior function of the hypothesized type.

### Step 5: Consider Mistyping
Review the commonly confused pairs above. If the type feels slightly off, test the most likely alternative type with the differentiating questions.

### ISTJ vs INTJ

Both are reserved, analytical, and systematic. The difference is Si (past-anchored, detail-oriented) vs Ni (future-oriented, pattern-seeking).

- **ISTJ** (Si-Te): Builds on proven experience. Values reliability, consistency, and established methods. Skeptical of untested approaches.
- **INTJ** (Ni-Te): Builds toward a strategic vision. Values innovation, efficiency, and long-term optimization. Skeptical of "we've always done it this way."

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "How do you feel about changing a system that's working well? Do you see it as unnecessary risk (ISTJ) or as an opportunity to make it even better (INTJ)?"
2. "When you think about the future, is it concrete and specific — based on extrapolating from what you know (ISTJ) — or more like a vision that feels certain even though you can't fully explain why (INTJ)?"
3. "Do you prefer to refine existing processes (ISTJ) or redesign them from scratch (INTJ)?"

### ENFP vs ESFP

Both are enthusiastic, people-loving, spontaneous types. The difference is Ne (abstract possibilities) vs Se (physical present).

- **ENFP** (Ne-Fi): Lives in the world of ideas and possibilities. Sees metaphors and hidden connections everywhere. Energy comes from brainstorming.
- **ESFP** (Se-Fi): Lives in the physical present moment. Sees beauty and opportunity in what's immediately real. Energy comes from action and experience.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When something excites you, do you describe it in metaphorical or abstract terms (ENFP), or in concrete, sensory terms (ESFP)?"
2. "Are you more energized by brainstorming ideas, or by doing things — physically engaging with the world?"
3. "Do you tend to start many projects and struggle to finish them because new ideas pull you away (ENFP), or do you get bored with things that aren't immediately stimulating (ESFP)?"

**Decisive differentiator:** Language. ENFPs naturally use metaphorical, abstract language ("it felt like falling into a kaleidoscope"). ESFPs use literal, vivid, sensory language ("the colors were incredible and the music was perfect"). This shows up consistently regardless of education or vocabulary.

### ISTP vs ISFP

Both are quiet, independent, and present-focused. The difference is Ti (impersonal logic) vs Fi (personal values).

- **ISTP** (Ti-Se): Analyzes how things work. Cool and detached under pressure. Motivated by mastery and understanding mechanisms.
- **ISFP** (Fi-Se): Creates beauty and meaning. Warm but private. Motivated by authenticity and personal expression.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When you see something beautiful, is your first instinct to understand how it works (ISTP) or to appreciate and feel it (ISFP)?"
2. "In a crisis, do you become calm and analytical (ISTP) or do you respond based on what feels right (ISFP)?"
3. "Is it more important that things make logical sense, or that they feel authentic?"

### ENTJ vs ENFJ

Both are charismatic leaders who drive toward goals. The difference is Te (systems/efficiency) vs Fe (people/harmony).

- **ENTJ** (Te-Ni): Leads through strategic competence. Organizes systems and people toward measurable outcomes. Can seem impersonal.
- **ENFJ** (Fe-Ni): Leads through inspiration and emotional connection. Develops people toward their potential. Can seem overly involved.

**Key differentiating questions:**
1. "When you lead a team, is your primary measure of success the results achieved or the growth of the people involved?"
2. "In a meeting, do you focus on advancing the agenda efficiently, or on making sure everyone feels heard?"
3. "When someone underperforms, is your first thought about the impact on the project or about what's going on with them personally?"

## Red Flags for Mistyping

- **Introvert who seems extraverted**: Many Introverts are socially skilled. Check energy source, not social competence.
- **Feeler who seems like a thinker**: Many Feelers, especially males, mask their feeling preference. Check what they VALUE in decisions, not how they PRESENT them.
- **Perceiver who seems organized**: IxTPs have strong internal logical systems (Ti). Check their relationship with external structure and closure.
- **Judger who seems flexible**: ENxJs can appear adaptable because of Se tertiary. Check if they feel better with closure or openness.
- **Anyone claiming to be "balanced"**: Most people lean one way. If they claim perfect balance on a dichotomy, dig deeper — the preference often shows under stress.

## Common Mistyping Patterns

### Most Over-Claimed Types
- **INFJ**: ~80% of people who self-type as INFJ are actually INFP, ISFJ, or ISFP. INFJ is the rarest type (~1-2% of population) but the most commonly claimed because the description sounds appealing. Always test rigorously.
- **INTJ**: Frequently over-claimed by INTPs (who may wish they were more decisive) and ISTJs (who may confuse Si detail-mastery with Ni vision).
- **ENTP**: Over-claimed by ENFPs who enjoy debate but actually argue from values, not logic.

### Most Under-Claimed Types
- **ISFJ/ISTJ**: These common types are under-claimed because descriptions sound "boring." Many self-typed INFJs are actually ISFJs.
- **ESFJ/ESTJ**: Similar — practical, common types that people resist identifying with.

### Gender Bias in Typing
Cultural expectations distort self-report:
- **Men** tend to over-report T (Thinking) and under-report F (Feeling) because emotional intelligence is culturally coded as feminine. An INFP man may present as INTP. Always check decision BASIS, not presentation.
- **Women** tend to over-report F (Feeling) and under-report T (Thinking) for the inverse reason. An INTJ woman may present as INFJ. Check what they PRIORITIZE in decisions.
- **The fix**: Ask about actual decisions and their reasoning process, not about self-perception. "Walk me through how you made that choice" reveals more than "are you logical or emotional?"

### Loop and Grip Mistyping
- **Dom-Tert loops**: When someone uses their 1st and 3rd functions while bypassing the 2nd, they can appear as a different type entirely. An INFJ in a Ni-Ti loop can look like an INTP. Check if the pattern is chronic or situational.
- **Grip states**: Under extreme stress, any type can look like its opposite. An ESTJ in Fe grip looks like an unhealthy Feeler type. Always ask "is this how you USUALLY are, or is this a stress response?"
