# Interview Questions: Comprehensive Human Profile

These questions, asked conversationally across multiple sessions, extract comparable qualitative insights to 8-10 personality assessments — with deeper cross-framework synthesis than any single instrument provides.

## Structure

The interview has 8 phases. Each phase maps to one or more assessment frameworks. Questions are grouped by the insight they extract, not the framework they map to.

**Estimated time**: 2-3 hours across 2-4 sessions (rushing produces shallow results).

### Diagnostic Approach

These questions are designed to **differentiate** between all possible types, not confirm a suspected one. As you interview:

1. Form hypotheses early but hold them loosely
2. Use `references/type-differentiation.md` to select follow-up questions that test between commonly confused type pairs
3. Track contradictions — they may reveal paradoxes (cross-framework) or aspirational answering
4. By the end of Phase 3, you should have strong hypotheses for I/E, S/N, and J/P
5. Phase 4-6 should clarify T/F and begin Enneagram/EQ typing
6. Phase 7-8 should confirm or revise all hypotheses

**Throughout the interview**, maintain a running internal assessment:
- I ◻ / E ◻ (confidence: low/medium/high)
- S ◻ / N ◻ (confidence: low/medium/high)
- T ◻ / F ◻ (confidence: low/medium/high)
- J ◻ / P ◻ (confidence: low/medium/high)
- A ◻ / T ◻ (confidence: low/medium/high)
- Enneagram hypothesis: ___
- DISC hypothesis: ___

## Phase 1: Energy & Social Orientation
*Maps to: MBTI (I/E), Big Five (Extraversion), DISC (Active/Receptive), EQ (Wellbeing)*

### Core Questions
1. **After a long, demanding day, what does recharging look like for you?** (Introversion vs Extraversion)
2. **Describe your ideal weekend — the one that leaves you feeling most restored.** (Energy source)
3. **When you walk into a room full of strangers at a social event, what's your internal experience?** (Social anxiety vs disinterest vs excitement)
4. **How many close friends do you have? How many would you call if something terrible happened at 2am?** (Social depth vs breadth)
5. **Do you tend to speak up in group discussions, or do you prefer to listen and process before sharing?** (Active vs Receptive)
6. **How do you feel about small talk? What about deep one-on-one conversations?** (Social energy preferences)

### Probing Follow-ups
- "When you say you need alone time, what are you actually doing during that time?"
- "Has anyone ever described you as shy? Do you agree with that assessment?"
- "Do you enjoy being the center of attention, or does it make you uncomfortable?"

### Context Needed
Tell me about a recent social situation that drained you, and one that energized you. What was different about them?

---

## Phase 2: How You Think & Process Information
*Maps to: MBTI (S/N), Big Five (Openness), VARK, Strengths (Researcher, Architect, Thinker)*

### Core Questions
7. **When you walk into an unfamiliar place — a new office, a friend's house, a restaurant — what do you notice first? Walk me through those first 30 seconds.** (Sensing: physical details — layout, temperature, colors. Intuition: overall vibe, what it reminds them of, what it means.)
8. **How do you learn something new best — by reading about it, watching someone do it, having someone explain it, or jumping in and trying it yourself?** (VARK)
9. **When you need to learn something complex, do you feel confident after one source of information, or do you need to cross-reference multiple sources?** (Multimodal learning)
10. **When you learn something new, do you trust your direct experience more, or do you trust patterns and theories more? Give me an example.** (Sensing: trusts direct experience, proven methods. Intuition: trusts patterns, frameworks, theoretical understanding.)
11. **Do you enjoy brainstorming and exploring possibilities, even if most ideas won't be practical?** (Openness)
12. **When you read instructions for assembling furniture or learning a new tool, do you read the manual first, or start building and refer to it when stuck?** (VARK: R vs K)

### Probing Follow-ups
- "If you had to teach someone else what you just learned, how would you do it?"
- "Do you take notes when learning? What do your notes look like — text, diagrams, bullet points?"
- "Do you prefer podcasts/audiobooks or written articles/books for learning?"
- "Have you ever felt frustrated because a teacher or trainer only used one method (e.g., lectures only, or reading only)?"

### Context Needed
Describe the last time you had to learn something genuinely difficult. Walk me through your process from start to finish.

## Phase 3: How You Work & Get Things Done
*Maps to: DISC (all dimensions), Big Five (Conscientiousness), Strengths (Get It Done domain), MBTI (J/P)*

### Core Questions
13. **Describe your ideal work environment — the one where you do your best work.** (DISC environment preferences)
14. **You're planning a two-week trip. Do you research and book everything in advance, or arrive with minimal plans and figure it out? How does each approach FEEL to you?** (Judging: closure feels good, openness feels anxious. Perceiving: openness feels freeing, closure feels limiting.)
15. **How do you feel about deadlines? Do they motivate you or stress you?** (Conscientiousness)
16. **When a project has unclear requirements, what do you do?** (Tolerance for ambiguity)
17. **Do you prefer to work alone or collaboratively? Under what conditions does each work best for you?** (Work style)
18. **How organized are your physical and digital spaces? Do you have systems for things?** (Structure/Methodical)
19. **When you start a project, do you tend to finish it? What makes you abandon something?** (Follow-through)
20. **How do you handle interruptions when you're focused on something?** (Pace preference)

### Executive Function Probes
These questions distinguish between disorganization as preference (P-type) vs capacity constraint (executive dysfunction). This distinction matters enormously for AI support strategies.

20a. **Do you WANT to be more organized but struggle to maintain systems, or are you genuinely comfortable with your current level of structure?** (Preference vs constraint)
20b. **When you have multiple tasks to do, can you prioritize them easily, or does deciding what to do first feel like its own task?** (Task initiation / prioritization)
20c. **Do you ever hyperfocus on something for hours, losing track of time and other obligations?** (Hyperfocus pattern — ADHD indicator)

### Probing Follow-ups
- "Do you tend to do things the established way, or do you look for better methods?"
- "How do you feel when someone changes the plan at the last minute?"
- "Do you take ownership of problems that aren't technically yours to solve?"
- "Would people describe you as fast-paced or steady-paced? Which do you prefer?"
- "Do you find starting tasks harder than continuing them once started?"

### Context Needed
Tell me about a project you're proud of completing. What made you stick with it? Now tell me about one you abandoned. What happened?

## Phase 4: How You Relate to Others
*Maps to: Big Five (Agreeableness), DISC (Communication/Conflict), EQ (Other Awareness, Empathy), Enneagram (wings, arrows), Strengths (Connect & Elevate)*

### Core Questions
21. **When a friend is upset, what's your instinct — to fix the problem, listen and validate, or give them space?** (Empathy style)
22. **Do you tend to pick up on other people's moods, even when they haven't said anything?** (Other Awareness)
23. **Has anyone ever told you that you take on other people's emotions? Do you agree?** (Empathy absorption)
24. **How do you handle conflict? Walk me through what happens internally when someone disagrees with you strongly.** (Conflict style)
25. **Do you find it easy or difficult to say no to people?** (Agreeableness/boundaries)
26. **In a team, do you naturally take the lead, support others, or observe and contribute when you see a gap?** (Team role)
27. **How do you give feedback to someone who isn't performing well?** (Communication style — direct vs diplomatic)
28. **Do you have a tendency to help people even when they haven't asked for it?** (Helper pattern)

### Attachment Style Probes
Attachment patterns fundamentally shape how people relate to others and to AI tools. These questions surface the pattern without clinical framing.

28a. **In close relationships, do you tend to worry about being abandoned or rejected, or do you feel pretty secure that people will stick around?** (Anxious vs secure attachment)
28b. **When things get emotionally intense in a relationship, is your instinct to move toward the person or to pull back and create space?** (Anxious: move toward. Avoidant: pull back. Secure: contextual.)
28c. **How easy is it for you to depend on other people? To let them help you?** (Avoidant attachment resists dependency. Secure is comfortable with mutual dependence.)

### Probing Follow-ups
- "When you sense someone is struggling, do you ever feel compelled to step in even if it's not your place?"
- "Do you ever feel resentful after helping someone? When?"
- "How do you feel when someone rejects your help or advice?"
- "Do you sometimes know what someone needs before they do?"

### Context Needed
Tell me about a relationship (friend, partner, colleague) where you gave too much. What happened? How did it end?

---

## Phase 5: What Drives You — Motivation, Values & Purpose
*Maps to: Schwartz Values, Enneagram (core fears/desires), Big Five (Motivation pattern), Strengths (Make an Impact)*

### Core Questions
29. **What matters most to you in life? If you could only keep three things, what would they be?** (Core values)
30. **What kind of work feels meaningful to you? What kind feels like it's wasting your life?** (Purpose/mission)
31. **How important is achievement to you? Do you feel driven to accomplish things, or are you more content to just be?** (Achievement value)
32. **How do you feel about rules and traditions? Are they important to follow, or more like guidelines?** (Tradition/Conformity values)
33. **What role does fairness and justice play in your life? How do you react when you see injustice?** (Universalism)
34. **How important is independence to you? What does having autonomy in your life look like?** (Self-direction/Power)
35. **Think about the last time your routine was completely disrupted — new job, move, life change. Was your first reaction closer to relief and excitement (new possibilities), or anxiety and disorientation (lost stability)?** (Stimulation vs Security — behavioral response, not stated preference)
36. **What does success look like to you? Has your definition changed over time?** (Achievement/Power)
37. **How important is it that your daily work aligns with your personal values?** (Values-orientation)

### Probing Follow-ups
- "Is there a cause or mission you feel called to pursue?"
- "Do you ever feel guilty about pursuing pleasure or enjoyment for its own sake?"
- "How do you feel about people who prioritize money and status above all else?"
- "Would you take a pay cut for more meaningful work? Have you?"

### Context Needed
Describe a time when you felt deeply fulfilled. What were the circumstances? Now describe a time when you felt like you were living someone else's life. What was different?

## Phase 6: Your Emotional Landscape
*Maps to: EQ (all factors), Big Five (Neuroticism), Enneagram (emotional patterns), MBTI (T/F)*

### Core Questions
38. **When you make important decisions, do you lead with logic or feelings? Can you give an example?** (Thinking vs Feeling)
39. **How well do you understand your own emotions in the moment? Can you usually name what you're feeling?** (Self-Awareness)
40. **When you're angry or upset, how do you handle it? Do you express it, suppress it, or something else?** (Emotional Control)
41. **How long does it take you to recover from a bad day or a major setback?** (Resilience)
42. **Do you experience emotions intensely? Do people ever tell you you're "too sensitive" or "too much"?** (Emotional range)
43. **What's your relationship with vulnerability? Is it easy or hard for you to show weakness to others?** (Vulnerability pattern — critical for E8 detection)
44. **When was the last time you cried? What triggered it?** (Emotional depth)
45. **Do you tend toward optimism or pessimism? How do you feel about that tendency?** (Optimism baseline)

### Identity Axis (Assertive vs Turbulent)
46a. **When you make a mistake at work, how long does it stay with you?** (Self-criticism duration — Turbulent dwells, Assertive moves on)
46b. **Is your sense of self-worth relatively stable, or does it fluctuate based on how things are going?** (Identity stability)
46c. **Are you harder on yourself than you are on other people?** (Self-compassion gap — Turbulent almost always says yes)
46d. **Does stress motivate you to work harder, or does it make you want to withdraw?** (Stress as fuel vs stress as drain)

### Probing Follow-ups
- "When you pick up on someone else's distress, does it affect your own mood?"
- "Have you ever been told you're hard to read emotionally?"
- "Do you have strategies for calming yourself down when emotions run high?"
- "Do you tend to worry about things that haven't happened yet?"
- "When someone praises you, do you accept it easily or immediately think of reasons you don't deserve it?"
- "Do you replay conversations in your head after they happen, analyzing what you said?"

### Context Needed
Think of the last time you felt overwhelmed by emotions — yours or someone else's. What was happening? How did you get through it?

### Diagnostic Notes
This phase is critical for T/F differentiation. Watch for:
- **Thinkers** who have high emotional awareness may appear as Feelers. Check their decision BASIS, not their emotional capacity.
- **Feelers** who have developed strong logic may appear as Thinkers. Check what they VALUE, not what they DO.
- The Assertive/Turbulent axis is independent of T/F — both Thinkers and Feelers can be either.

## Phase 7: Power, Control & Vulnerability
*Maps to: Enneagram Type 8 detection, DISC (Drive), Strengths (Decisive, Leader), Values (Power)*

**Note**: This phase surfaces the deepest patterns. It requires trust and should not be rushed. These questions detect Enneagram core type, particularly the 8-2-4 tritype pattern.

### Core Questions
46. **How do you feel about being in charge? Do you naturally take control of situations?** (Power/leadership drive)
47. **What happens when someone tries to control or micromanage you?** (Autonomy need — E8 indicator)
48. **Do you see yourself as a protector of others? Who or what do you feel compelled to protect?** (E8 protective instinct)
49. **What does strength mean to you? What does weakness mean to you?** (E8 core belief system)
50. **When you were a child, did you feel like you had to be strong or grow up fast?** (E8 childhood origin)
51. **Do you ever feel like you're fundamentally different from other people — like you don't quite fit?** (E4 pattern)
52. **Is there a part of you that feels like something is missing from your life?** (E4 longing)
53. **Do you sometimes feel like you know what's best for someone, even better than they do?** (E1/E2 pattern)
54. **How do you handle it when people you've helped don't appreciate it or take your help for granted?** (E2 shadow)

### Instinctual Variant Probes (Enneagram)
The instinctual stacking (sp/so/sx) changes how each Enneagram type manifests. These three questions identify the dominant instinct.

54a. **When you're stressed, what's the first thing you worry about — your physical safety and comfort (health, money, home), your social standing and belonging (reputation, community, role), or the intensity of your closest relationships (connection, intimacy, passion)?** (sp/so/sx triage)
54b. **In a group, are you more focused on making sure your personal needs are met, on how the group dynamic is working, or on finding one person to connect with deeply?** (sp/so/sx in social context)
54c. **What feels most threatening to you — losing your resources and security, losing your place in the group, or losing an intense personal bond?** (sp/so/sx core fear)

### Probing Follow-ups
- "When was the last time you felt truly vulnerable? How did you handle it?"
- "Do you ever feel like you need to be the strongest person in the room?"
- "Have you ever been described as intimidating? Were you surprised by that?"
- "What would it take for you to fully trust someone?"
- "Do you ever test people to see if they can handle your intensity?"

### Context Needed
Tell me about a time when you felt powerless. What did you do? How did it affect you?

## Phase 8: Growth Edges & Self-Perception
*Maps to: All frameworks — synthesis questions that reveal self-awareness and growth areas*

### Core Questions
55. **What are you best at? What comes most naturally to you?** (Conscious strengths)
56. **What do you struggle with most? What would people who know you well say you need to work on?** (Conscious weaknesses + blind spots)
57. **What patterns do you notice in your life that you wish you could change?** (Recurring themes)
58. **How do you handle failure? What's your inner dialogue when something goes wrong?** (Perfectionism, self-esteem)
59. **What's your relationship with perfectionism? Do you hold yourself to very high standards?** (E1 pattern, common growth edge across many types)
60. **When you're at your worst — stressed, exhausted, overwhelmed — who do you become?** (Disintegration pattern)
61. **What does personal growth mean to you? How actively do you pursue it?** (Growth orientation)
62. **If you could change one thing about yourself, what would it be?** (Core growth edge)

### Probing Follow-ups
- "Do you ever feel like you're wearing a mask that people like more than the real you?"
- "What are you most afraid of other people finding out about you?"
- "Do you think you're harder on yourself than you are on others?"
- "What would the younger version of you think of who you've become?"

### Context Needed
Imagine someone who knows you extremely well — better than anyone — writing a letter about who you really are, including the parts you don't show. What would it say?

## Synthesis Prompts (Post-Interview)

After completing all phases, the interviewer should identify:

### Cross-Framework Patterns
- Where do the results converge? (Multiple frameworks pointing to the same trait)
- Where do they contradict? (Paradoxes like INFJ + E8 — these are the most revealing)
- What surprised you? (Patterns the person may not be aware of)

### The Five Synthesis Questions
1. **Who is this person at their core?** (1-2 sentences)
2. **What are they afraid of?** (Core fears across frameworks)
3. **What do they want most?** (Core desires across frameworks)
4. **What's holding them back?** (Growth edges and blind spots)
5. **What's the single most important thing for them to understand about themselves?** (The insight that would make the biggest difference)

## Interview Guidelines

### Pacing
- Don't ask questions in rapid succession. Let answers breathe.
- Follow interesting threads. The best data comes from follow-ups, not scripted questions.
- If someone gives a short answer, ask "Can you tell me more about that?" or "What do you mean by that?"

### Trust Building
- Phases 1-3 are lower vulnerability. Start there.
- Phases 6-7 require trust. Don't rush to them.
- If someone deflects a question, note the deflection — it's data. Circle back later.

### Red Flags for Aspirational Answering
People often answer who they *want* to be, not who they *are*. Watch for:
- "I should..." instead of "I do..."
- Answers that sound like textbook definitions
- Contradictions between stated preferences and described behavior
- Ask for specific examples when answers feel rehearsed

### What the AI Cannot Observe Directly
These normally come from test instruments with scaled responses:
- Precise numerical scores (the interview produces ranges, not exact numbers)
- Reaction time / implicit associations
- Response patterns across hundreds of forced-choice items

The interview compensates by going *deeper* on fewer dimensions, surfacing qualitative richness that numeric instruments miss.
