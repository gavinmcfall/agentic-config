# Worked Example: Complete Profile

This example shows the full pipeline from interview data through synthesis to AI Working Context for a fictional person, "Sam Chen."

## Raw Interview Data (Excerpts)

### Phase 1 — Energy & Social Orientation
> **Q1 (Recharging):** "After a long day I need to be alone. Not antisocial — I just need quiet. I'll put on headphones and play guitar or read. If someone talks to me before I've had that reset time, I get irritable."
>
> **Q3 (Room of strangers):** "I scan the room for someone who looks like they don't want to be there either. If I find them, we'll end up having the deepest conversation at the party. I hate working the room."
>
> **Q4 (Close friends):** "Three. Maybe four. But those three know everything about me. I'd call any of them at 2am."

**Running assessment:** I ☑ (high confidence) — classic introvert energy pattern. Social depth over breadth.

### Phase 2 — Thinking & Processing
> **Q7 (Unfamiliar room):** "The vibe. Immediately. I walked into my friend's new apartment and before I noticed the furniture I thought 'this place feels like her.' Then I noticed the plants and books and thought 'of course.'"
>
> **Q10 (Experience vs patterns):** "I definitely trust patterns. I read three books about woodworking before I ever picked up a chisel. My partner thinks I'm insane — she just starts building things."

**Running assessment:** N ☑ (high confidence) — leads with impressions and patterns, not physical details. Learns through theory first.

### Phase 3 — Work Style
> **Q14 (Trip planning):** "I research obsessively. Hotels, restaurants, walking routes. I have spreadsheets. When my partner suggested we 'just wing it' on our Italy trip I had a mild panic attack. But — I also hate when the plan becomes a prison. I need the plan to EXIST, but I want permission to deviate."
>
> **Q20a (Organization as preference or struggle):** "I want the systems. I build the systems. The systems work for about two weeks and then I stop using them. Then I feel guilty and build a new system. It's a cycle."

**Running assessment:** J ☑ (medium confidence) — needs closure but the system-abandonment cycle suggests possible executive function constraint, not P-type preference. Probe further.

### Phase 4 — Relating to Others
> **Q21 (Friend upset):** "My instinct is to fix it. I can see exactly what they should do. But I've learned — painfully — that people usually want to be heard first. So I sit on my hands and listen. It takes effort."
>
> **Q24 (Conflict):** "My heart rate goes up immediately. I feel the other person's anger before I process my own position. I'll often concede in the moment to stop the tension, then realize later I was actually right and feel frustrated."
>
> **Q28b (Emotional intensity in relationships):** "I pull away. Not because I don't care — because I care too much and need to process it alone before I can be useful."

**Running assessment:** F ☑ (medium confidence) — Fe-like pattern (reads others' emotions first), but the fix-it instinct and delayed self-awareness suggest possible T with high empathy. Need Phase 6 to clarify. Attachment: likely avoidant-leaning.

### Phase 6 — Emotional Landscape
> **Q38 (Logic vs feelings in decisions):** "I lead with logic — or I think I do. But when I trace back big life decisions, they were all feeling-based. I chose my career because it 'felt right,' not because I ran a cost-benefit analysis. I just tell myself it was logical afterward."
>
> **Q43 (Vulnerability):** "Extremely hard. I'll share vulnerability through humor or through helping others with the same problem. Direct vulnerability — 'I'm struggling and I need help' — almost never."

**Running assessment:** T/F revised → F ☑ (high confidence). Decision BASIS is feeling even though PRESENTATION is logical. Classic F-type who has developed strong analytical skills.

### Phase 7 — Power, Control & Vulnerability
> **Q47 (Someone controls you):** "I become a wall. Quiet, compliant on the surface, but internally I'm already planning my exit. I don't fight — I leave."
>
> **Q50 (Childhood):** "I was the mediator. My parents fought and I learned to read the room before I could read books. I became the person who made everything okay for everyone else."
>
> **Q54a (Instinctual variant):** "Resources and security, without question. Losing my home or financial stability is my deepest fear. Social standing? I genuinely don't care."

**Running assessment:** Enneagram hypothesis: Type 9 (conflict avoidance, mediator childhood, wall-building) with possible 2 wing (caretaker pattern). Instinctual: sp-dominant.

## Synthesis

### Cross-Framework Convergence

| Trait | MBTI evidence | Big Five evidence | Other evidence |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Introverted | Energy from solitude | Low Extraversion (~30%) | DISC: Receptive |
| Intuitive | Leads with impressions | High Openness (~70%) | VARK: Visual/Read |
| Feeling-based decisions | Decisions traced to values | High Agreeableness (~65%) | EQ: High Other-Awareness |
| Closure-seeking | Plans obsessively, needs plan to exist | Moderate Conscientiousness (~55%) | J preference |

**Type hypothesis: INFJ** (Ni-Fe-Ti-Se)
- Ni: Pattern-recognition, "just knows" the vibe, convergent thinking
- Fe: Reads others' emotions first, conflict avoidance for harmony, mediator role
- Ti: Analytical presentation layer, fix-it instinct, system-building
- Se inferior: Uncomfortable with pure physical spontaneity

### Cross-Framework Paradox

**The Mediator Who Walls Off**: Sam's Fe drives them to read and manage others' emotional states (mediator childhood, concedes in conflict), but their avoidant attachment means they withdraw when emotional intensity gets too high. They're exquisitely tuned to others' emotions but protect themselves by disappearing. This creates a cycle: attune → absorb → overwhelm → withdraw → guilt → re-engage.

### Cross-Framework Gap

**System-Building vs System-Maintaining**: High closure need (J) combined with system-abandonment cycle suggests executive function constraint. Sam's desire for structure is genuine (not aspirational answering — they described genuine panic at "winging it") but sustained execution breaks down. This is not a J/P mistype — it's a capacity gap.

### The Five Synthesis Questions

1. **Who is Sam at their core?** A deeply empathetic pattern-reader who absorbs others' emotional states while keeping their own inner world carefully guarded, driven by a need to make everything okay for everyone while quietly protecting their own resources and stability.

2. **What are they afraid of?** Conflict (Enneagram 9), losing security/resources (sp-dominant), being truly seen and found insufficient (avoidant attachment), and losing the structures that hold their life together (executive function anxiety).

3. **What do they want most?** Inner peace and harmony (E9), deep meaningful connections without losing themselves (INFJ), a sense that their life has purpose and meaning (Ni-Fe), and enough security to feel safe (sp).

4. **What's holding them back?** The withdraw-when-it-matters pattern. Sam's emotional intelligence is their greatest asset, but the avoidant shutdown under intensity means they leave exactly when their insight is most needed — in their own relationships and in their own self-understanding.

5. **What's the single most important thing for Sam to understand?** That their habit of "going wall" isn't protecting them — it's preventing the deep connection they actually crave. Their Fe reads others perfectly; their avoidant pattern prevents others from reading them. The vulnerability they avoid IS the bridge.

## Output Profile (Abridged)

### One-Sentence Summary
A quietly perceptive introvert who absorbs others' emotions through an empathy radar they can't turn off, then protects themselves by withdrawing behind a logical, self-sufficient exterior — all while craving the deep connection their withdrawal prevents.

### Personality Architecture
- **Cognitive Style**: INFJ (Ni-Fe-Ti-Se). Leads with pattern recognition, processes through social harmony lens, presents through analytical frame.
- **Behavioral Style**: DISC S/C — supportive, steady, detail-conscious when engaged.
- **Emotional Architecture**: EQ Other-Awareness ~75%, Self-Awareness ~60%, Emotional Control ~45%. Absorbs more than they can regulate.

### Motivational Core
- **Enneagram**: Type 9w2, sp/sx stacking. Seeks harmony and peace; helps others as a way of maintaining connection without direct vulnerability.
- **Core Fear**: Conflict, disconnection, resource loss.
- **Core Desire**: Inner peace, meaningful purpose, security.

### Executive Function Profile
- Strong desire for systems and structure (genuine J preference, not aspirational)
- System maintenance breaks down after ~2 weeks — suggests working memory or sustained attention constraint
- Hyperfocus capable when deeply interested
- Task initiation is harder than continuation
- **AI implication**: Needs external scaffolding, gentle check-ins, and systems designed for restart rather than continuous maintenance

### The Paradoxes
1. **Empathy Radar + Emotional Withdrawal**: Absorbs others' states with extraordinary accuracy, then walls off when intensity rises — the very moment their insight matters most.
2. **System Builder + System Abandoner**: Genuinely needs and creates structure, but cannot sustain it — capacity constraint, not preference.
3. **Fix-It Instinct + Conflict Avoidance**: Sees exactly what people should do, then concedes in the moment to avoid tension. Later frustration cycle.

## AI Working Context: Sam Chen

### Communication Preferences
**How to communicate:**
- Lead with the pattern or insight, not the data. Sam processes top-down.
- Allow processing time. Do not expect immediate responses to important questions.
- Be direct about information but diplomatic about feedback. Sam reads tone extremely well — bluntness without warmth reads as hostility.
- When Sam gives a short or deflecting answer, they're processing, not disengaged. Ask a follow-up rather than repeating.

**What to avoid:**
- Rapid-fire questions or demanding immediate commitments
- Framing things as urgent when they aren't — triggers anxiety without productive action
- Over-explaining rationale when Sam has already grasped the point (Ni picks it up fast)

### Decision Support
**How Sam decides:** Appears analytical but actually decides by values/feeling, then constructs logical justification afterward. Don't trust Sam's stated reasoning process — track what they actually choose.

**How to help:**
- Present 2-3 options with clear trade-offs rather than open-ended questions
- When Sam is stuck, ask "What feels right?" rather than "What makes sense?" — this cuts through the analytical overlay to the actual decision mechanism
- Give space after presenting options. The answer will come, but not immediately.

### Task & Work Patterns
**How Sam works best:** Structured environment with autonomy within the structure. Needs the plan to exist but wants permission to deviate.

**Where they need support:**
- System maintenance — design workflows that expect restarts rather than continuous adherence
- Task initiation — "What's the smallest first step?" is more helpful than "Here's the full plan"
- Breaking hyperfocus when it's time to switch tasks
- External accountability (gentle, not punitive)

### Emotional Awareness
**Stress signals:** Withdrawal, shorter responses, increased logical/analytical framing (Ti takes over from Fe), loss of humor.

**How to respond:**
- When stressed: Do NOT push for emotional disclosure. Offer practical help and quiet presence.
- When overwhelmed: Reduce scope. "What's the one thing that matters right now?"
- When self-critical: Reflect back evidence of their actual impact on others (Fe responds to this).
- Never say: "Just relax" or "Stop overthinking it" — invalidates the processing they need.

### Motivation & Growth
**Core drivers:** Purpose, meaningful connection, security, helping others grow.

**Growth edges (handle with care):**
- The withdrawal pattern: Gently notice when it's happening. "You seem like you're pulling back — want to talk about it, or do you need space?" gives them the choice their avoidant pattern needs.
- Direct vulnerability: Don't push. Model it instead.
- Self-compassion: Sam extends grace to others effortlessly but withholds it from themselves.

### Learning & Development
**How Sam learns:** Theory first, then application. Needs to understand the framework before trying the skill. Multimodal but leans visual and reading. Cross-references multiple sources before trusting any single one.

**AI teaching approach:**
- Explain the "why" and the pattern before the "how"
- Provide written reference material, not just verbal explanation
- Don't repeat what Sam has already grasped — they'll signal understanding quickly (Ni)
- When teaching something hands-on, let them watch first, then try

**Avoid:** "Just try it and see what happens" — this triggers the Se-inferior discomfort. Sam needs enough theoretical grounding to feel safe experimenting.
