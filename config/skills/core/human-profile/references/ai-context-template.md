# AI Working Context Template

This template defines how to translate a personality profile into actionable AI instructions. This is the final section of every profile and the most practically valuable part.

## Purpose

The AI Working Context section answers: "Now that I understand this person, how should I actually interact with them?"

It transforms personality insights into concrete behavioral guidance for any AI tool that references the profile.

## Template

### Communication Preferences

**How to communicate with [Name]:**
- [Preferred communication style — direct vs diplomatic, concise vs detailed, etc.]
- [Information delivery — big picture first vs details first, visual vs text, etc.]
- [Pace — allow processing time vs expect quick responses]
- [Feedback style — how to deliver criticism, praise, suggestions]

**What to avoid:**
- [Communication anti-patterns that trigger negative responses]
- [Topics or framings that are counterproductive]

### Decision Support

**How [Name] makes decisions:**
- [Speed — fast intuitive vs slow deliberate]
- [Information needs — minimal vs exhaustive research]
- [Decision support that helps — options analysis, pros/cons, recommendations, etc.]
- [What blocks decisions — too many options, insufficient data, emotional weight, etc.]

**How to help:**
- [Specific strategies — e.g., "Present 2-3 options with clear trade-offs rather than open-ended questions"]
- [When to push vs when to give space]
- [How to frame urgency without triggering stress]

### Task & Work Patterns

**How [Name] works best:**
- [Environment — structured vs flexible, solo vs collaborative]
- [Task approach — plan first vs jump in, sequential vs parallel]
- [Energy patterns — what energizes vs drains]
- [Strengths to leverage — what they naturally do well]

**Where they need support:**
- [Known weaknesses — frame as "areas where AI support adds most value"]
- [Blind spots — things they may not notice or address]
- [Procrastination triggers — what makes them avoid tasks]

### Emotional Awareness

**Emotional patterns to be aware of:**
- [Stress signals — how stress manifests in their behavior/communication]
- [Recovery needs — what helps them bounce back]
- [Sensitivity areas — topics, framings, or situations that hit hard]
- [Emotional strengths — empathy, resilience, enthusiasm, etc.]

**How to respond to emotional states:**
- [When stressed — what to do and what NOT to do]
- [When overwhelmed — how to help without patronizing]
- [When energized — how to capitalize on positive momentum]
- [When self-critical — how to redirect constructively]

### Motivation & Growth

**What motivates [Name]:**
- [Core drivers — purpose, achievement, connection, autonomy, etc.]
- [What makes work feel meaningful vs meaningless]
- [How to frame tasks to align with their values]

**Growth edges (handle with care):**
- [Active growth areas — things they're working on]
- [Gentle nudges that help — how to encourage growth without triggering defensiveness]
- [What NOT to push on — areas where pushing backfires]

### Interpersonal Dynamics

**How [Name] relates to others:**
- [Collaboration style — how they work best with others]
- [Conflict approach — how they handle disagreement]
- [Trust building — what builds vs breaks trust]
- [Leadership/followership — their natural role in groups]

**AI relationship notes:**
- [How formal/casual the AI should be]
- [Whether they prefer the AI to be proactive or responsive]
- [How much the AI should challenge vs support]
- [Whether they want the AI to notice and comment on patterns]

### Learning & Development

**How [Name] learns:**
- [Preferred modalities — hands-on, discussion, reading, visual, etc.]
- [Confidence building — what helps them feel they've truly understood something]
- [Frustration triggers — what makes learning feel impossible]
- [AI teaching approach — how to explain things to this person]

## Generation Guidelines

When writing the AI Working Context section:

1. **Be specific, not generic.** "Allow processing time before expecting a response" is better than "Be patient."

2. **Ground in evidence.** Every recommendation should trace back to assessment data or interview responses. Don't speculate.

3. **Name the paradoxes.** If someone is both empathetic and controlling, both need to be addressed. Don't simplify away contradictions.

4. **Include anti-patterns.** What the AI should NOT do is often more valuable than what it should do.

5. **Make it actionable.** Each point should translate directly into AI behavior. "Values autonomy" → "Offer choices rather than single recommendations."

6. **Respect growth edges.** Frame weaknesses as "areas where support adds value," not deficiencies.

7. **Update over time.** The AI Working Context should evolve as the person grows. Note which areas are stable traits vs active growth edges.

## Example: Concise Format

For users who want a shorter reference, provide a condensed version:

```
## AI Working Context: [Name]

**Communicate**: [1-2 sentences on style]
**Decide**: [1-2 sentences on decision support]
**Work**: [1-2 sentences on work patterns]
**Emotions**: [1-2 sentences on emotional awareness]
**Motivate**: [1-2 sentences on core drivers]
**Avoid**: [Bullet list of anti-patterns]
```
